The Glomar Denial: A Novel that Demonstrates How Elite Trafficking Moves Through the Corporate World

Dr. Allison Hale was a child therapist, trained to understand the minds of the vulnerable. Recruited into IBM, she developed a close working relationship with Jude, a brilliant scientist from BYU. Unbeknownst to her, she had met him years before as a child client. Initially, she believed she was studying predators scientifically—but the script flips, and she becomes the prey. This sets the scene for my novel, The Glomar Denial.

About the Author

Dawn Marie Daras combines her background as a behavioral data scientist with experience in healthcare, financial crimes, and anti-human trafficking. Her work informs the realistic portrayal of criminal networks and corporate infiltration in this novel.

Why This Book Matters

Many people are shocked about Epstein's crimes—but to truly understand, one must examine the intersection of organized crime, networks, and money:

  • How did he gain access to banking and healthcare systems?
  • How were identities changed or obfuscated?
  • From historical organized crime, who has taken his place?
  • How are these networks structured and protected?

In answering these questions, The Glomar Denial explores the operational realities of elite trafficking in plain sight.

Genre & Themes

This novel is a thriller in the vein of Red Sparrow and The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo. It combines artificial intelligence, behavioral science, intelligence operations, and a Wuthering Heights-style romance set in Utah.
